const prem_id = db.orders.aggregate([{$group:{ _id:"$customer_id",total_orders:{$sum:1}, total_spending:{$sum:"$total_amount"}}},{$match:{$or:[{total_orders:{$gt:5}},{total_spending:{$gt:1000}}]}}]).toArray().map(d=>d._id);
db.customers.updateMany({_id:{$in:prem_id}},{$set:{is_premium:true}});
First, we group the orders by customer_id and calculate the total number of orders and total spending for each customer. 
Then, we select customers who have more than 5 orders or total spending greater than 1000. 
Next, we extract the customer_id values from these results and store them in an array. 
Finally, we use updateMany to update these customers by setting their is_premium field to true.

const youngids = db.customers.find({age: {$lt: 25}}).toArray().map(d=>d._id);
db.orders.aggregate([{$match: {customer_id: {$in: youngids}}},{$unwind: "$items"},{$group: {_id: "$items.category"}}])
This task is completed in two steps. First, we retrieve the IDs of customers whose age is less than 25. Then, we use these IDs to filter orders and apply $unwind on the items array to expand them, followed by grouping by items.category to obtain distinct categories.
